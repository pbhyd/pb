Buying Guide

How to Choose the Right Baking Tools

Why We Share Our Tool List

Every home baker eventually asks the same question: "What do you actually use?" Rather than answering one WhatsApp message at a time, we've put together this page — every oven, mixer, nozzle, board, chocolate brand, flavour, colour and gadget that goes into the cakes we bake every single day at Pure Bakes in Hyderabad. Nothing here is a random Amazon listing; every product has either replaced something inferior in our kitchen or earned a permanent place in it.

Ovens, Mixers & Blenders

The right OTG or stand mixer changes everything about your baking consistency. A 52–60 litre OTG with motorised rotisserie gives you the capacity to bake multiple tiers at once, while a reliable stand mixer with a copper motor saves your wrists from hand-whisking buttercream for twenty minutes. We list the exact models that have stood up to daily commercial-level use in our kitchen.

Piping Nozzles & Decorating Tools

A good piping nozzle set is the difference between buttercream that looks hand-piped by a professional and buttercream squeezed out in a hurry. Stainless steel nozzles hold their shape far longer than plastic, and a reliable turntable is essential — smoothing fondant on a cake that isn't rotating evenly is one of the most common frustrations for home bakers.

Cake Boards, Tins & Boxes

Presentation starts before the first slice is cut. A sturdy cake board prevents warping under multi-tier weight, and well-made boxes protect delicate fondant work during transport — critical given Hyderabad's heat and traffic. We look for boards thick enough not to bow under 2–3kg of cake and boxes with secure lock-corner designs.

Compound & Couverture Chocolate

Not all chocolate behaves the same way once it's melted. Compound chocolate is easier to work with for everyday ganache and drips, while couverture (true Belgian chocolate with proper cocoa butter content) gives a glossier finish and deeper flavour for premium cakes. We use both depending on the cake — and list the exact brands we trust for each.

Flavours, Colours & Edible Decor

Concentrated oil-soluble flavour essences let us build distinctive flavours without watering down batter, and gel-based colours like Colourmist give vivid, true-to-shade results without affecting texture — unlike liquid food colouring. Edible lustre dust and airbrush solutions are what give finished cakes their signature shimmer.
